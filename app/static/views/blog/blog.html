Hi world! My name is Forrest. I'm a 25 year old guy from the US, leaving behind a high-paying job at a hedge fund for long-term, low-budget travel around the world. But let's back up - how did I end up here?


I grew up in Kansas. Maybe it's because there wasn't much to do there and we had to create our own entertainment, maybe it's just who I am, but as a kid I developed a strong urge for adventure. My friends and I would go explore where we could, riding our bikes around and occasionally getting into trouble for our shenanigans. My adventure-seeking continued to grow with time. I picked up pole vaulting in high school, which was a thrilling outlet for my energy. In college, I joined a great group of guys in a fraternity. My degree in neuroscience really helped me appreciate both the experiences and the dangers of the substances I experimented with. An amazing, fun, transformative, and surreal four years later, I graduated and was ready to move on.


And so I entered the "real world," a bright-eyed kid armed with a strong sense of optimism and a newly acquired degree. Instead of following the traditional neuroscience path of becoming a doctor or doing research, I decided this was my chance to try out a job, make some money, and see what else was out there. I was really lucky and managed to get a job at Goldman Sachs, so I was off to NYC! Say what you want about the vampire squids, but I have a ton of respect for Goldman. I was surrounded by talented, driven people, and they taught me how to really work. At the same time, I got to live in one of the most amazing cities I've ever been to (more on that later)! I had a great group of friends, enough money for the expensive lifestyle, and the youthful endurance needed to go out in the city that never sleeps.


An intense year later, I again got lucky and was offered a job at a quantitative hedge fund in Connecticut. I accepted, jumping at the chance to learn from former Harvard professors and some of the most respected people in the quant world. But as I settled in, I realized I wasn't happy. Yes, I was making more money than a 23 year old probably should and was living what looked like a nice life. Black cars drove me from Connecticut back to NYC where I still lived. I could (and am embarrassed to say, sometimes did) spend over $500 a night on dinner or going out. But I wasn't happy. I could feel my optimism, idealism, and passion for living life draining away, and that sucked.


Unsurprisingly, finance seemed like a place where most people worked just for the money, without some underlying passion or sense of purpose. What was I creating? I made money into more money and I guess that helped some people. Why did I have to sit at this desk for ten hours a day? This was the 21st century; all of my work could be done remotely. I could be out seeing the world! As these thoughts gnawed at me, I decided I needed to go. Life wasn't about making money so I could buy nice things, then needing to make even more money to buy even nicer things. I wanted to live an inspired life filled with amazing experiences and help others do the same. I wanted to surround myself with passionate, inspired people who were living the dream!


So here I am. Leaving behind the safety of a good, stable job for the adventure of the road. Working to create something I believe in, to help others see the world that I'm lucky enough to experience.
